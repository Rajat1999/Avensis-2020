Robowars

Get ready for these metallic gladiators

Challenge statement

To create a robot capable of duelling with another robot of same weight class.

Event Description

The robots will duel in an arena of given dimensions within a stipulated time period. A robot will be victorious when it is able to push its counterpart out of the arena boundary /making it immobile or one of them is deemed as ‘broken’ by the event heads.

Rounds

Round I
1. Two Bots will battle in each round
1.1. A round will be 5 minutes long.
2. For a robot to win it must push its counterpart out of the arena or make it immobile for more than 30 seconds
2.1. A bot will be declared immobile if it is unable to exhibit linear motion of at least 2 inches within a time period of 30 seconds
Round II
1. Two Bots will battle each other 7 minutes
2. For a robot to win it must push it’s counterpart out of the arena or make it immobile for more than 25 seconds
2.1. A bot will be declared immobile if it is unable to exhibit linear motion of at least 2 inches within a time period of 25 seconds.
If no robot is departed from the arena in the given amount of time, the round shall continue until one opponent is departed out of arena.

Bot Specifications

1. Weight categories: -
a. Light weight – up to 10kgs
b. Heavy weight—up to 40kgs

Main Rules and Regulations of Event

1. Maximum limit for the clash of combatants inside arena is 5 minutes for the first round and 7 minutes for the second round.
2. The bot will have a safety inspection, done by the event heads, before it is put in the competition.
3. If a team is not present within five minutes of calling, then the opposing team will be automatically declared the winner.
4. A bot will be declared immobile (out) if it cannot exhibit linear motion of at least 2 inches within 30 seconds.
5. The bot cannot be split into two subunits. Two distinct parts cannot be connected by a flexible cable. They will be considered separate units.
6. Event heads decision will be final and binding. Arguing against them will result in elimination of the team.
7. Rules are flexible and can be changed according to Event heads.
8. Voltage supply – 0-12/0-24 V
9. Robot size: - 40 X 40 X 40 (Light weight) & 80 X 80 X80 (Heavy weight).
10. Robot can be Walking or Rolling type.
11. Team Limit –max. 4 members per team.
12. Any college/school students can participate in the event.

Eligibility Criteria

Each member of the team should have a valid ID card of issuing authority at the time of Event.Otherwise the participation will not be considered.

RESTART AND TIMEOUT

A maximum of 1 Timeout of 1 Minute each may be taken in the second round only.

Roborugby

GET READY TO BATTLE

Event Details

A match of Robo Rugby involves two small robots and some balls of different colours. It is an event in which robots try to score a point by hitting the balls into the scoring zone at both end points of playing table. Every coloured ball clutches different points. One robot is assigned to each team and the position of each ball at the end of game governs the final score and hence the winner. There are 3 rounds which are listed below:

Rounds

Round 1: Collect the balls
In the first round, participants have to find the balls placed in the arena and tackle the maximum number of balls towards the goal point in fixed amount of time.
Round 2: Tackle the hurdles
In the second round, participants will have to take the ball from start to end point, facing some hurdles; bots need to tackle them all in minimum time possible and complete the round
Round 3: BOt Vs BOT
The Last round will include a rugby match between two bots, Participants need to compete their opponent bot and score the goal.

Specifications

Size of the bot: 30 X 30 X 20 (in cms)
Minimum length of wire allowed is 10m
Maximum length of extension allowed is 10cms
Maximum power supply: 24(volts)

Rules and Regulations

1. Each team can consists a maximum of 4 members.
2. Each team should have a unique bot.
3. Cross college teams are allowed.
4. The event will be totally time based. There will be additional time penalties that will be declared before the event starts.
5. There should be proper discipline maintained during the competition. No hassle will be there.
6. The Right spirit of participation is expected from the participants.
7. In the Event we will be providing the teams with only AC (220V, 50Hz) power supply source.
8. The final decision will be of EVENT HEADS and binding on all the participants without any exceptions.

Winning Criteria

The game is based on the scores.
The team with maximum score will win.
The factors affecting the result of the event are:
▪ Accuracy
▪ No. of balls collected
▪ No. of penalties
Those who believe will be Champions. Rock Solid and Ready to Rumble.
So, gear up your bots for the most exhilarating event of AVENSIS 2020 and together make it a huge success!!

Carnage

TO FINISH FIRST, YOU MUST FIRST FINISH

CHALLENGE

“Manoeuvre your bots till the finish line in the fastest time”
Design a manually controlled ROBOT that has the capacity to cover maximum distance in shortest possible time, challenging the hurdles and be one of the best opponents. Think your robot can overcome any obstacle-big or small in the least of time. If so get it on the track and let the game begin. And bear in mind that maximizing RPM does not make you winner but winners are those who have good presence of mind, sharpness and practice.

PHASES IN THE EVENT

The events consists of two rounds
● ROUND 1
Qualification on the basis of time.
The competition is based upon time trail system. This round will act as qualifying round for each team.
The top team to qualifying makes it on the basis of time trials.
● ROUND 2
Elimination on the basis of perfection and time.
This will act as the final round. All about final round will be notified on the final day.

RULES AND REGULATIONS

1) Each team can have a maximum of 4 members.
2) Each team should have unique robot to participate in the event.
3) Cross college teams are allowed.
4) The Right spirit of participation and discipline (during the competition) is expected from the participants.
5) The decision of the EVENT HEADS of CARNAGE will be final and binding on all the participants without any exceptions.
6) The event will be totally time based and additional time penalties will be declared before the event starts.
7) It is the participant’s responsibility to know about all the rules and then start to perform.
8) Scores of any team will not be declared and will be kept confidential

BOT SPECIFICATIONS

1) The maximum dimension of the robot can be 30 x 30 x 30 cm (l x b x h).
2) The robot may be wired or wireless.
3) The length of the wire (for wired bots) should be long enough to cover the whole track and wire should remain slack during the complete run (atleast 10 m) .
4) Max weight must not exceed 6 kg.
5) The power supply will be provided maximum up to 24Volt, DC socket for 220Volt will be provided by the organizing team.

PENALITIES AND DISQUALIFICATION

1) Restart during the run will incur a penalty leading to deduction of points.
2) Going through a hurdle out of order will result in a penalty.
3) Disturbing or illegal touching the hurdle while passing through will lead to penalty.
4) Breaking or vandalizing any part of the field will result in instant disqualification. This includes breaking any hurdle pole or getting the floor excessively dirty such that the numbers or patterns on the ground cannot be read.

ELIGIBILITY

All students with a valid identity card of their respective educational institutes are eligible to participate in the event.

Line following robots

Heroes aren't born. They're built

Description

Plan of Action for line following bot Event Details about the event: 1. A line follower robot is basically a robot designed to follow a ‘line’ or path predetermined by the programmer. 2. This line or path may be as simple as a physical white line on the floor or as complex path as per marking schemes e.g. embedded lines, magnetic markers and laser guide markers. 3. In order to detect these specific markers or ‘lines’, various sensing schemes can be employed. 4. These schemes may vary from simple low cost line sensing circuit to expensive and sophisticated vision systems. 5. From the industrial point of view, line following robot has been implemented in semi to fully autonomous plants.

CHALLENGE STATEMENT

build an autonomous robot which is capable of following a line. This line can be a white line on a black background or a black line on a white background .The robot will cover the track following this line.

TASK

The autonomous robot will begin its run from an initial marked position in a dark isolated room. It will follow the line strip beneath it and would cover the track. The robot has to decide its path by sensing the line. The objective is to cover the entire track in least possible time with sufficient accuracy

ROUNDS

ROUND 1:
1. There will be a single track.
2. There is no such limit on the time of completion of the bot run however the team which completes the run in minimum time qualifies for the next round.
3. Maximum of 8 hand touches (but with time penalty) and one hand pick (with extra time penalty) is allowed throughout the round in order to adjust its position if it is heading wrong.
4. There will be one checkpoint and if it gets skipped, additional penalty will be charged accordingly.
5. The team cannot make any programming changes in the course once the bot starts its run.
6. Calibration time will be provided before the competition .
ROUND 2:
1. Track complexity increases this time.
2. Each team will have maximum of 5 minutes to complete the track.
3. Maximum of 6 hand touches (but with penalty) and 2 hand picks (with extra penalty) are allowed throughout the round in order to adjust its position if it is heading wrong.
4. There will be four checkpoints and maximum two can be skipped but penalty will be charged accordingly.
5. The team cannot make any programming changes in the course once the bot starts its run.

Main Rules and Regulations of Event

1. Decision of chief organizers and event heads is final and every participant has to accept it.
2. Round 1 is completing the track efficiently and in less time.
3. Clearing round 1 in an average minimum stipulated time ensures qualification to the next round.
4. Each team can have maximum of 4 members.
5. Each team should have unique participants i.e. it cannot have any common member.
6. Cross college teams are allowed. 7. The Right spirit of participation and discipline (during the competition) is expected from the participants.

EVENT

1. There will be a line present in the arena. This line can be white on black background or black on white background.
2. The thickness of line will not be the same.
3. The track will have breaks in it i.e. gaps.
4. In case the robot is not able to cross the gap they can reposition it but marks will be deducted for it.

RESTART AND TIMEOUT

1. A maximum of 1 Timeouts of 5 Minutes each may be taken in the second round only.
2. A penalty will be rewarded for each Timeout taken by the team and the robots will start from the start zone.
3. The participants can have a maximum of 2 restarts for rounds. A penalty will be imposed for each restart.

Robomaze

don't just gaze, change your pace and win the race

Objective

The robots ( ultrasonic sensor controlled) will have to start from one end of the maze, make the required turns and move till the far end of the maze in minimun time and with minimum penalties possible. Robots will have to qualify first round so as to make their way for the final maze i.e., final round

Details of the event

1. A Robo Maze comprises of a sensor controlled robot which tracks the correct path in maze and completes it.
2. It detects obstacles with the help of its sensors and hence moves in a different direction.
3. Various changes can be made in the working code of the robot to overcome various complexities in the maze.
4. The competition is conducted in such a way that the best bot which solves all the complexities of the maze in the minimum time wins.

ROUNDS

Round 1:
1. Round 1 comprises of a basic maze which tests through some of the complexities.
2. The track is a simple unidirectional maze, and a few pockets.
3. The participants clearing the round 1 with maximum score will move toward the round 2 of the event.
4. The number of qualifying teams will be decided by the number of teams partipating.
Round 2:
1. This round will have higher level of difficulty than round 1.
2. This round will be knockout tournament, the winning team goes ahead and the losing team goes home.
3. Time penalty would be imposed more strictly.

Rules and Regulations

1. Decision of the organisers is final and binding, though appeals will be entertained.
2. Each team can have a maximum of 4 members.
3. Each team should have unique particiants i.e, they cannot have any common members.
4. Cross college teams are allowed.
5. The right spirit of participation and discipline (during the competition) is expected from the participants.
6. After the maze is revealed, teams will have limited time to make the required modifications.
7. Time penalties will be given in cases like hand touch, hand pick, etc. These penalties are decided by the organisers and are revealed at the time of event.
8. Checkpoints are present of both tracks. Team can reset their bot at checkpoint but with some penalty.
9. 1st and 2nd team will be decided by the final match of round 2.

Disqualification

Only sensor controlled (IR sensor or ultrasonic sensor) can participate. Remote controlled bots will not be allowed.
*The right to disqualify participating teams resides with Event Heads.
The participants are requested to have a healthy competition, with sportmanship and positive attitude so as to make the event more entertaining.
